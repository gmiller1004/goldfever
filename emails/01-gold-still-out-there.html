Every week somebody tells me, "Tom, ain't all the gold gone by now?"

I just laugh and pull the little vial out of my pocket — pickers I found last Saturday afternoon on ground that's been worked for a hundred years.

Rivers move gold. Floods scrub fresh bedrock. Every year new shiny shows up for folks with a pan and a little know-how. You don't need a TV crew. You need a shovel, a gold pan, and a creek that'll let you in.

That's Chapter 1 in your guide: gold is still out there, waiting on regular people like us.

The 1872 Mining Law still gives everyday prospectors a fair shake on a whole lot of public ground — hand panning, detecting, small sluices — when you use common sense and respect the resource.

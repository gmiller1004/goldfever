The single most important thing my dad George "Buzzard" Massie ever taught me — and the thing I repeated on every Gold Fever shoot:

Private property is private. Always ask permission first. Every single time.

But here's the part that'll make you smile: when you ask the right way, folks say yes way more than no — and half the time they walk you to the honey hole themselves.

These two sentences worked from Georgia to Alaska:

"Hi there, I'm Tom. My family and I love panning for gold — just hand tools, no mess. Would you mind if we spent a couple hours on your creek today? We'll fill every hole and leave it cleaner than we found it."

Smile. Mean it. Offer to share any nice pieces (most laugh and say keep it). You'll be amazed.

One of my favorite days was in Georgia — Mr. Johnson in hip boots with us, telling stories about his granddad's two-ounce nugget from the '30s. We left with full pans, homemade pickles, and a standing invitation.

If there's one thing I'm prouder of than any jar of nuggets on my shelf, it's watching an entire family catch the fever at the same time.

Gold fever is a family disease — and thank the good Lord there ain't no cure.

My kids grew up with pans in their hands. My daughter Kia caught the detecting bug harder than anybody — one afternoon in Last Chance Canyon her coil screamed, she dug six inches, and pulled up a nugget rough and yellow as butter. She looked up with eyes brighter than the gold and hollered, "Dad! I got one!" That little piece still sits on my desk.

I've seen shy teenagers turn into chatterboxes after their first flake. I've seen husbands and wives knee-deep in the same hole, laughing at each other covered in mud. I've seen grandkids hug their granddad's neck because "we found GOLD, Papa!"

Calm creeks, easy access, no big iron required — just sunshine and patience. Throw a picnic on the bank and watch the magic happen.

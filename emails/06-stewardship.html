Before we go any further, I need to talk straight with you about something that burns in my heart.

We're the luckiest folks on God's green earth — we get to chase shiny in some of the prettiest country there is. That privilege comes with a big responsibility. If we don't take care of the land, the water, and the folks who own it, the gates slam shut and they don't open easy.

Dad drilled it into me: "Son, you leave every place better than you found it, or you don't deserve to come back."

That's the code we lived by on Gold Fever, and it's the code I want every one of you to tattoo on your brain:

  • Fill every hole — detector scoop or test pit, rake it smooth.
  • Pack out every scrap of trash — yours and anybody else's.
  • Don't tear up banks or live trees — fish expect their rocks back.
  • Be quiet and respectful — we're guests in God's cathedral.

I've seen claims closed forever because one knucklehead left a mess. I've seen ranchers open brand-new stretches because a family filled every hole, picked up beer cans, and left a thank-you note on the porch.

Guess which one keeps the fever alive for the next generation?

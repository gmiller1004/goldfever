Chapter 11 is the dead-simple plan I used on every Gold Fever shoot for twenty years. Here's the kit — under a hundred bucks if you're starting from zero:

  1. 14-inch gold pan (green or blue — gold shows up bright)
  2. Folding shovel or army entrenching tool
  3. Classifier screen (½-inch mesh on the pan)
  4. Snuffer bottle + vial
  5. Small pry bar for bedrock cracks

Add gloves, sunscreen, water, snacks, and a trash bag (we'll talk stewardship soon).

The rhythm: dig below the water line → classify → pan → tease color into the snuffer. First pan might be all black sand. Fifth pan might have twenty flakes. Fiftieth pan might have a picker that makes you holler.

Find inside bends, bedrock cracks, and behind boulders where heavy gold drops out. If somebody official rolls up, smile big and show you're legal — I've never had trouble when I was doing it right.
